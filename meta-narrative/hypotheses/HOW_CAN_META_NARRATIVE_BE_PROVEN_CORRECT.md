# How Can Meta-Narrative Be Proven Correct?

## Overview
This document explores approaches and criteria for supporting the validity of the meta-narrative framework.

## Evidence and Validation Strategies
- **Predictive Power:** Show that meta-narrative models can predict or explain real-world outcomes, behaviors, or system dynamics that are otherwise hard to model.
- **Internal Consistency:** Demonstrate that the equations and protocols are logically consistent and produce coherent results across scenarios.
- **Empirical Case Studies:** Apply the framework to diverse domains (teams, stories, organizations, games) and document successful alignment, recovery, or transformation.
- **Comparative Analysis:** Compare meta-narrative predictions and interventions to those from other frameworks (e.g., systems theory, psychology, game theory) and show unique or superior results.
- **Reproducibility:** Enable others to use the framework and achieve similar results in their own contexts.
- **Peer Review:** Invite critique, testing, and refinement from other researchers, engineers, and storytellers.

## What Would Count as Proof?
- Consistent, repeatable success in modeling, guiding, or improving complex systems.
- Clear, documented cases where meta-narrative interventions lead to positive, measurable change.
- Acceptance and adoption by a community of practitioners and researchers.
